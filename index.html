close project

craftili, 2021


goals ↓

craftili is an online marketplace for customizable products. The client’s approach prioritized humanizing online transactions, bridging the gap between creator and consumer. My aim was to design a landing page that explained the platform’s features and goals while maintaining accessibility. This page would be active prior to and during the launch of the platform.


process ↓

Bespoke items are typically associated with affluence and thus exclusivity, so it was important that the design not come across as too cold or luxurious. At the same time, I was aware that the target demographic would be a ‘hip’ crowd whose sensibilities would not be catered to by existing cookie-cutter retailers. With this in mind, I ensured that the design was modern, clean, and neutral. I figured that the featured sellers on the site would be apt appeal to the fringe interests of potential users (the sellers are not pictured in the displays above), and that an understated design would do best for a marketplace-style platform. Because the core concept of the platform is not radically new, my approach to novel appeal relied more on visual tactics. This is why I decided to employ a video background. It is both optically stimulating, and also conveys the company’s emphasis on humanity and craftsmanship. I stitched together a montage of stock photos to send to the client, and then remotely directed the filming process of the actual featured clients. Similarly, I implemented parallax motion on scroll.


note ↓

*I did not develop the website for craftili.

STC @ UCLA
Design
2021
close project

STC @ UCLA, 2021


overview ↓

I was tasked with redesigning the website and branding for the STEM transfer community at UCLA. I worked with the existing site, which was hosted on WIX, to improve the user experience and to modernize the org’s visual identity.


process ↓

There were two constraints I worked under: I was required to keep the website on WIX, and I had to adhere to UCLA’s branding principles. The former made the process easier, as I enjoy designing with limitations and adapting an old aesthetic into a new form. This was not the case for the latter; I found that WIX as a site builder presented limitations in areas I did not appreciate- I found myself unable to flexibly design the site structure and found that I couldn't exactly implement the UX system I had in mind for the organization.


note ↓

I also created graphics for various social media accounts, and redesigned the org's logos using typefaces from velvetyne.

Learnidy
Design
2021
close project

Learnidy, 2021


overview ↓

I designed a call-to-action window for the digital education tool Learnidy, to be triggered when the user exits the platform after making changes to their work.


process ↓

It was important to incentivize signing up by reminding the user of what they had done on the platform, so I decided to include a diagram containing an example of a full schedule to emphasize the work that would be lost if they were to exit without signing up.


note ↓

While discussing with the founders, I noted how the specific appeal of a platform or application (i.e. convenience or effectiveness) affects what type of incentive structure should be implemented.
